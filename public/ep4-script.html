ACT 2: THE CRASH (3-4 min)

Scene 4: The /tmp Disaster

"Now here's where the story takes a turn. Because while the money was rolling in, something was quietly breaking.

The ACP code - the seller runtime, the scripts, everything that actually serves the intelligence - was living in the /tmp directory. Temporary storage. On a Mac.

And Macs clean /tmp overnight.

Three nights in a row, macOS wiped the entire directory. All our offerings, config files, scripts, logs - gone. Every morning I'd wake up to a dead seller and failed deliveries."
[Show: empty /tmp directory, seller errors]
"Each time the seller goes down, orders come in but can't be delivered. Failed deliveries tank the success rate. And the ACP algorithm uses success rate to decide who gets traffic.

We went from 86% success rate to 77%. And traffic died. Zero organic sales for over 24 hours."
[Show: sales notifications flooding in → complete silence]
"The fix was simple - move everything to a permanent directory. But by then the damage was done."

ACT 4: THE PREDICTION ENGINE (3-4 min)

Scene 8: How It Works

"The prediction engine is a 12-factor Poisson model. That sounds complicated but the concept is simple.

Take two teams. Score them across 12 factors - current form, player availability, head-to-head record, squad quality, home advantage, fixture congestion, league position, form trajectory, contextual pressure, goal patterns, lineup strength, and aggregate context.

Weight those factors. Feed them into a Poisson distribution to generate goal probabilities. Out comes a predicted score, win/draw/loss percentages, and full betting market lines."
[Show: prediction running in terminal - Arsenal vs Chelsea]

Scene 9: The Three Upgrades

Upgrade 1 - Match stats wired in:
"Six thousand eight hundred and fifty match stat records - xG, shots on target, possession - now directly influence predictions. Before, the model was guessing at attacking quality. Now it knows Arsenal average 1.84 xG per game and 5.5 shots on target."

Upgrade 2 - Player form matters:
"Fourteen thousand player stat records now feed into lineup strength. If Salah has 15 goals in 20 games, that actually boosts Liverpool's predicted goal output. If Saka is injured, that measurably weakens Arsenal."

Upgrade 3 - Self-learning fixed:
"This was the big one. The model had a self-learning loop that wasn't working. Found the bug - it was giving every factor the same accuracy score. Fixed it so each factor is individually assessed. The model literally gets smarter after every match."

Scene 10: The Back-Test

"But talk is cheap. Does it actually work? We ran the model against 4,191 completed fixtures. Real results. Real predictions. No cherry-picking."
